Convert Transform Across DCC with Matrix Operation

2022-08-16 learning log math, maya, unity, unreal

I don’t want the math, take me to the code! (Click Here)

Introduction

Have you ever transformed assets between DCCs?

I love the flexibility and reliability of .fbx (until USD takes over), the importing and exporting from one software to another is trouble-free. Now, there’s a lot going on in the fbx SDK, let’s discuss one of its aspect: converting Transformation

Have you noticed the different transform values when one asset is imported to a different package? Here’s an example:

Different transform

Here I have some ‘axis’ loaded in Maya, Unity and Unreal. Looking at the center ‘axis’, it has the same transformation value for all three of them:

translation rotation scale
Maya, Unity, Unreal x: 0, y: 0, z: 0 x: 0, y: 0, z: 0 x: 1, y: 1, z: 1

However, when we look at the ‘axis’ located on the top-right, the transformation will give you completely different result:

translation rotation scale
Maya x: -1.31, y: 0.33, z: 0.19 x: -335, y: -12, z: 98 x: 1, y: 1, z: 1
Unity x: -1.31, y: 0.33, z: 0.19 x: 7, y: -27, z: -102 x: 1, y: 1, z: 1
Unreal x: -1.31, y: 0.19, z: 0.33 x: 149, y: 75, z: 123 x: 1, y: 1, z: 1

What makes them so different (mostly rotation), and how can we compute those values?

Coordinate System in DCC

The fundamental reason for this is that, even though all DCCs use a Cartesian coordinate system, they have different axis alignment, and rotation direction.



  • Up Direction: For Unreal, positive Z is considered as the world up direction, instead of positive Y in Maya and Unity.

  • Handed System: dictate what direction of rotation: for left-handed system, positive rotation about the axis is clockwise, and for right-handed system, it’s counter-clockwise (same way as how your hand would curl, hence the name. learn more here)

Finally, let’s also get the easiest part out of the way, translation and scale values are usually compute by swapping values between axis. This is understandable, because moving an object up in the world of Unreal is positive Z direction, whereas in Maya and Unity, it’s positive Y direction, so inheritly, the Z and Y values are swapped.

Quaternion vs. Matrix vs. Euler angles

Rotation lies in non-Euclidean space.

  • Euler angles: being the most popular representation of rotation across all DCCs. it is easy for us to understand. However, it can cause ambiguity and artifacts because of the rotation order and axis independence.

    • α\alphaα is rotation about the first axis

    • β\betaβ is rotation about the second axis

    • γ\gammaγ is rotation about the third axis


  • Quaternion: game engines use quaternion internally, because it is data compact and computational efficient. It’s bigger advantage comes from sequential rotation as it produces predictable results. It is, however, very hard for average users to comprehend.

    A quaternion consists of a scaler part and a vector part: q=[s,(x,y,z)]s,x,y,z∈Rq = [s, (x, y, z)] s, x, y, z \in Rq=[s,(x,y,z)]s,x,y,z∈R or. q=s+ix+ij+kzq = s + ix + ij + kzq=s+ix+ij+kz where i2=j2=k2=ijk=−1i^2 = j^2 = k^2 = ijk = -1i2=j2=k2=ijk=−1


  • Matrix: capable of representing affine transformation such as: translation, rotation and scaling, also space switching, change of basis which we are doing most of them today.

    Mtransform=[α11α12α13α14α21α22α23α24α31α32α33α340001] M_{transform} = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha_{11} & \alpha_{12} & \alpha_{13} & \alpha_{14}\\ \alpha_{21} & \alpha_{22} & \alpha_{23} & \alpha_{24}\\ \alpha_{31} & \alpha_{32} & \alpha_{33} & \alpha_{34}\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} Mtransform​=⎣⎢⎢⎡​α11​α21​α31​0​α12​α22​α32​0​α13​α23​α33​0​α14​α24​α34​1​⎦⎥⎥⎤​

Methods

The method I used are all fundamental matrix math, before going into the code, I want to establish the steps to convert transform from one DCC to another.

  1. From the source package, retrieve translation, rotation and scale (tsource,rsource,ssourcet_{source}, r_{source}, s_{source}tsource​,rsource​,ssource​)

  2. Compose a transformation matrix: MsourceM_{source}Msource​

  3. Convert to a transformation matrix in the target software coordinate system using a Change of Basis matrix: McobM_{cob}Mcob​

  4. Decompose the transformation matrix: MtargetM_{target}Mtarget​

  5. Post modification of the target translation, rotation and scale value such as flipping and swapping (ttarget,rtarget,stargett_{target}, r_{target}, s_{target}ttarget​,rtarget​,starget​)
